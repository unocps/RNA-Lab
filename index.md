The **Robotics, Networking, and Autonomous Intelligence (RNA) Laboratory** at the University of Nebraska at Omaha develops intelligent cyber-physical systems that seamlessly integrate physical autonomy, edge intelligence, and robust networking.

Our research bridges the gap between high-level machine learning models and real-world physical constraints, focusing on autonomous driving, humanoid robot communication, and self-optimizing networks.

---

### Research Core
* **Autonomous Intelligence:** Deep Reinforcement Learning (DRL), Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), and multi-agent systems.
* **Intelligent Robotics:** Humanoid locomotion, sensor fusion, and human-robot collaboration.
* **Network & Systems:** Congestion control, digital twins, and resource-constrained Edge AI.
